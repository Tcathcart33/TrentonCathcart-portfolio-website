Site Control PLC Project

Led end-to-end execution of a PLC-based jail control system designed for accessibility in a correctional facility, including accommodations for blind and deaf inmates. Personally responsible for project quoting, system design, and hands-on panel fabrication. Designed and implemented control logic where duress inputs triggered visual alerts at cell locations, enabling rapid identification by staff. Developed multi-modal communication systems using color-coded LED indicators to convey time-of-day information for deaf individuals, and timed audible buzzer sequences to relay equivalent information for blind individuals. Scope included PLC I/O design, 120VAC and 24VDC power distribution, intercom integration, and full system architecture. Produced complete engineering drawing packages including floor plans, one-line diagrams, enclosure layouts, and field wiring schematics. Panels were physically built and wired in-house, ensuring seamless alignment between design and real-world implementation in a high-reliability, mission-critical environment.
